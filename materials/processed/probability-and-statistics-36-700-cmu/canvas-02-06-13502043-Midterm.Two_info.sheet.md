## Page 2
### Content
* (Parametric inference continued):
    * the Maximum Likelihood Method (important!) including knowing the main properties of maximum likelihood estimators, *asymptotic normality*, *Fisher information* and its significance in parameter estimation, efficiency, equivariance and the Delta method
    * Cramer-Rao Bound
    * parametric vs nonparametric bootstrap
* Hypothesis testing:
    * general concepts (important!): test statistic, types of errors, p-values, size and level of a test, the Neyman-Pearson paradigm, null distribution, power function, interpretation and limitations of significance tests, statistical versus scientific significance
    * equivalence between tests and confidence sets
    * one and two-sided Wald tests
* Bayesian inference:
    * frequentist versus Bayesian inference
    * prior and posterior distributions
    * Bayesian updating/conjugate priors (definition)
    * posterior mean and mode
* Decision theory:
    * loss function and the risk of an estimator
    * maximum risk and minimax rule (general definitions + know that the Bayes rule with constant risk is minimax)
    * Bayes risk and Bayes rule/estimator (general definitions + know that the Bayes rule under squared-error loss is the posterior mean)
    * posterior risk
    * admissibility (definition)
