## Page 2
### Content
- Properties of expectations, variances and covariances of random variables; the “Rule of the Lazy Statistician”; **conditional expectation and conditional variance**
- Using **probability inequalities** to bound quantities that are otherwise hard to compute
- **convergence of a sequence of random variables** (in probability, in distribution and in quadratic mean); relationship between types of convergence, convergence properties under transformations
- **Law of Large Numbers and CLT**; when and how to use these laws
- the Delta method

* **Parametric Inference:**
    - **General concepts in statistical inference:** point estimation, **confidence sets**, consistency and bias of an estimator, MSE, parametric vs. non-parametric methods (definitions)
    - the Method of Moments
    - the **Maximum Likelihood Method** including properties of maximum likelihood estimators, asymptotic normality, **Fisher information** and its significance in parameter estimation, efficiency, equivariance and the Delta method
    - Cramer-Rao Lower Bound
    - parametric and non-parametric bootstrap (basic ideas)

* **Bayesian inference:**
    - frequentist versus Bayesian inference
    - prior and posterior distributions
    - Bayesian updating/conjugate priors (definition)
    - posterior mean and mode

* **Decision theory:**
    - loss function and the risk of an estimator
    - maximum risk and minimax rule
    - Bayes risk and Bayes rule/estimator

* **Hypothesis testing:**
    - **General concepts:** simple vs composite hypotheses, test statistic, types of errors, p-values, **size/level of a test**, the Neyman-Pearson paradigm, **null distribution, power function**, interpretation and limitations of significance tests, statistical versus scientific significance
    - Equivalence between **tests** and **confidence sets**
    - One and two-sided Wald tests (that assume asymptotic normality)
    - **Likelihood-ratio test**; degrees of freedom; Wilk’s theorem
    - Neyman-Pearson lemma
    - Pearson’s $\chi^2$ test for multinomial data
    - permutation testing

* **Nonparametric Density Estimation and Regression (general concepts/ideas):**
    - Pros/cons of parametric vs nonparametric models.
