### Kernel Regression

Let
$$\widehat{r}(x) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i(x)Y_i,$$
where
$$w_i(x) = \frac{K\left(\frac{x-X_i}{h}\right)}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} K\left(\frac{x-X_i}{h}\right)}, \quad \text{and, say, } K(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp(-x^2/2).$$

### Choosing the Smoothing Parameter

The prediction risk is
$$R(h) = \mathbb{E}(Y - \widehat{r}_n(X))^2,$$
which can be decomposed as
$$R(h) = \sigma^2 + \int b^2(x)f(x)dx + \int v(x)f(x)dx,$$
where
$$b(x) = \mathbb{E}(\widehat{r}(x)) - r(x) \quad \text{and} \quad v(x) = \mathbb{E}(\widehat{r}(x) - \mathbb{E}(\widehat{r}(x)))^2.$$

1. Derive this result.
2. What is $f$?
3. What is $r$?

### Analysis of the Regressogram

The integrated risk is:
$$R(\widehat{r}, r) = \int b^2(x)dx + \int v(x)dx \leq L^2h^2 + \frac{\sigma^2}{nh}$$

1. Derive these results.
2. What would the corresponding result be for the prediction risk?

The optimal bandwidth is:
$$h = \left( \frac{\sigma^2}{2nL^2} \right)^{1/3},$$
so the minimum risk is:
$$R(\widehat{r}, r) \leq \frac{2L^{2/3}\sigma^{4/3}}{n^{2/3}} = 2 \left( \frac{L\sigma^2}{n} \right)^{2/3}.$$

1. Derive the two results.
2. Plot $n^{-1}$, $n^{-2/3}$, and $n^{-4/5}$ against $n$ on the same graph. What is this plot supposed to illustrate?

### pD Non-parametric Regression

Suppose that the $\beta^{th}$ derivative of $r(x)$ is bounded, and we are in $d$-dimensions. In this case
$$R(\widehat{r}, r) = O\left(n^{-2\beta/(2\beta+d)}\right)$$

In linear regression, the rate of convergence is typically something like:
$$R(\widehat{\beta}, \beta) = O\left(\frac{d}{n}\right).$$

Assume that the 2nd derivative of $r^*(x)$ is bounded.

1. Plot $n^{-2\beta/(2\beta+d)}$ and $dn^{-1}$ against $n$ on the same graph, for $d = 1$. Observe.
2. Plot $n^{-2\beta/(2\beta+d)}$ and $dn^{-1}$ against $n$ on the same graph, for $d = 100$. Do you see what you expected to see?
