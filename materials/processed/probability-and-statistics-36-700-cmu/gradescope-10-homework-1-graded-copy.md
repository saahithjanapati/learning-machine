By Axiom 1, we know $P(\emptyset)$ is non-negative. For contradiction, assume $P(\emptyset) > 0$.
Note that by definition, $\Omega$, and $\emptyset$ are disjoint, so we have (by Axiom 3)
$$P(\emptyset \cup \Omega) = P(\Omega) = P(\emptyset) + P(\Omega) = 1 + P(\emptyset) > 1.$$
But this contradicts axiom 2. So, it must be true that $P(\emptyset) = 0$. $\blacksquare$

---

$P(A \subset B) \implies P(A) \le P(B)$

Let us define $C$ as $B \setminus A$.
Note $A$ and $C$ are disjoint (by definition).
By Axiom 3, we have
$P(A \cup C) = P(B) = P(A) + P(C)$. But if $P(A) > P(B)$, we have
$P(B) < P(A) + P(C)$ (since $P(C) \ge 0$ by axiom 1). So, $P(A) \le P(B)$. $\blacksquare$

6. $A$ and $B$ are independent events means $P(A \cap B) = P(A) \cdot P(B)$. Show that $A, B^c$ are independent events.

WTS that $P(A \cap B^c) = P(A) \cdot P(B^c)$

Note, $A = (A \cap B) \cup (A \cap B^c)$. Since $(A \cap B)$ is disjoint from $(A \cap B^c)$, we know
$P(A) = P(A \cap B) + P(A \cap B^c)$.
Note, $P(A \cap B^c) = P(A) - P(A \cap B)$
$P(A \cap B^c) = P(A) - P(A) P(B)$ (since $A, B$ are independent)
$P(A \cap B^c) = P(A) (1 - P(B))$
$P(A \cap B^c) = P(A) P(B^c)$

So, $A$ and $B^c$ are also independent. $\blacksquare$
