4. [5 pts] Short Answer:
Consider the function $f(x, y) = 3x^2 + 2y^2 + axy$ where $a, x, y \in R$. For what values of $a$ (if any) is $f$ convex, and for what values of $a$ (if any) is $f$ concave?

$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 6x + ay$
$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x \partial y} = a$
$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2} = 6$

$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 4y + ax$
$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y \partial x} = a$
$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2} = 4$

$H_f = \begin{pmatrix} 6 & a \\ a & 4 \end{pmatrix}$

$x^T H_f x = \begin{pmatrix} x_1 & x_2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 6 & a \\ a & 4 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \end{pmatrix}$
$= \begin{pmatrix} x_1 & x_2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 6x_1 + ax_2 \\ ax_1 + 4x_2 \end{pmatrix}$
$= 6x_1^2 + ax_1x_2 + ax_1x_2 + 4x_2^2 \ge 0$
$= 6x_1^2 + 2a(x_1x_2) + 4x_2^2 \ge 0$
$a(x_1x_2) \ge -3x_1^2 - 2x_2^2$

For convexity: $24 - a^2 \ge 0 \implies a^2 \le 24 \implies -\sqrt{24} \le a \le \sqrt{24}$.
For concavity: None, None.
