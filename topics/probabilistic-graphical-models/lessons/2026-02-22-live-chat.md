Yes. Using your course notation exactly:

Goal (sum-product BP on acyclic factor graph):
- compute exact marginals for variables and factors.

Messages:
1. Variable -> factor
$$
\mu_{i\to\alpha}(x_i)=\prod_{\alpha' \in \mathcal{N}(i)\setminus \alpha}\mu_{\alpha'\to i}(x_i).
$$
Meaning:
- variable $i$ sends to factor $\alpha$ the product of all other incoming factor messages.

2. Factor -> variable
$$
\mu_{\alpha\to i}(x_i)=\sum_{\mathbf{x}_\alpha:\mathbf{x}_\alpha[i]=x_i}
\psi_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)\prod_{j\in\mathcal{N}(\alpha)\setminus i}\mu_{j\to\alpha}(\mathbf{x}_\alpha[j]).
$$
Meaning:
- keep $x_i$ fixed,
- multiply factor potential with incoming messages from other variables in that factor,
- sum out the other factor variables.

Beliefs (unnormalized):
$$
b_i(x_i)=\prod_{\alpha\in\mathcal{N}(i)}\mu_{\alpha\to i}(x_i),
$$
$$
b_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)=\psi_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)\prod_{i\in\mathcal{N}(\alpha)}\mu_{i\to\alpha}(\mathbf{x}_\alpha[i]).
$$

Normalize to get marginals:
$$
p_i(x_i)\propto b_i(x_i),\qquad p_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)\propto b_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha).
$$

Execution on tree:
- initialize messages,
- leaves -> root pass,
- root -> leaves pass,
- compute beliefs and normalize.

## Response 19

User asked:
> can you make it a bit more in-depth?

Assistant response:
Yes. Deeper version in exact course notation.

Notation dictionary:
- $i$: variable node index.
- $\alpha$: factor node index.
- $\mathcal N(i)$: factor neighbors of variable node $i$.
- $\mathcal N(\alpha)$: variable neighbors of factor node $\alpha$.
- $\mathbf{x}_\alpha$: assignment to all variables in factor $\alpha$.
- $\mathbf{x}_\alpha[i]$: component of that assignment at variable $i$.

What message $\mu$ *means* on a tree:
- $\mu_{i\to\alpha}(x_i)$ is a summary from the subtree on the $i$ side of edge $(i,\alpha)$, as a function of fixed value $x_i$.
- $\mu_{\alpha\to i}(x_i)$ is a summary from the $\alpha$ side of the edge, also as a function of fixed $x_i$.
- Each message is a local dynamic-programming table, not a probability by itself.

Why variable -> factor is a product:
$$
\mu_{i\to\alpha}(x_i)=\prod_{\alpha' \in \mathcal{N}(i)\setminus \alpha}\mu_{\alpha'\to i}(x_i).
$$
Intuition:
- once $x_i$ is fixed, different neighboring subtrees become conditionally independent on a tree,
- so their contributions multiply.

Why factor -> variable is product then sum:
$$
\mu_{\alpha\to i}(x_i)=\sum_{\mathbf{x}_\alpha:\mathbf{x}_\alpha[i]=x_i}
\psi_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)\prod_{j\in\mathcal{N}(\alpha)\setminus i}\mu_{j\to\alpha}(\mathbf{x}_\alpha[j]).
$$
Intuition:
- multiply local compatibility $\psi_\alpha$ with incoming summaries from other variables in that factor,
- then sum over unknown local variables in factor $\alpha$ while keeping $x_i$ fixed.

Tiny concrete factor example (binary pair):
- factor $\alpha$ connects $(i,j)$.
- Then
$$
\mu_{\alpha\to i}(0)=\psi_\alpha(0,0)\mu_{j\to\alpha}(0)+\psi_\alpha(0,1)\mu_{j\to\alpha}(1),
$$
$$
\mu_{\alpha\to i}(1)=\psi_\alpha(1,0)\mu_{j\to\alpha}(0)+\psi_\alpha(1,1)\mu_{j\to\alpha}(1).
$$

Beliefs are local reconstructions after messages arrive:
$$
b_i(x_i)=\prod_{\alpha\in\mathcal{N}(i)}\mu_{\alpha\to i}(x_i),
$$
$$
b_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)=\psi_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)\prod_{i\in\mathcal{N}(\alpha)}\mu_{i\to\alpha}(\mathbf{x}_\alpha[i]).
$$

Normalization:
$$
p_i(x_i)=\frac{b_i(x_i)}{\sum_{x_i}b_i(x_i)},\qquad
p_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)=\frac{b_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)}{\sum_{\mathbf{x}_\alpha}b_\alpha(\mathbf{x}_\alpha)}.
$$

Message schedule (tree):
- initialize $\mu_{i\to\alpha}=1,\ \mu_{\alpha\to i}=1$,
- pass leaves -> root,
- pass root -> leaves,
- each node sends on an edge only after receiving all incoming messages on its other edges.

Why exact on trees:
- no loops means each message corresponds to a disjoint subtree summary,
- dynamic-programming decomposition is exact (no double counting).
