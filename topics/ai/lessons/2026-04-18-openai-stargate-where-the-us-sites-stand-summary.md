# OpenAI Stargate: where the US sites stand

Source note: [materials/processed/ai/openai-stargate-where-the-us-sites-stand.md](../../../materials/processed/ai/openai-stargate-where-the-us-sites-stand.md)

## Core claim

Epoch AI's report argues that Stargate has moved from announcement-stage ambition to real physical build-out. The key signal is that all seven US sites now show active development, with Abilene already partially online and the other sites advancing through permitting, foundations, framing, and power planning.

## What the article is actually tracking

This is not a general news recap. It is a site-status report for an industrial-scale AI infrastructure program. The article focuses on:

- current vs projected capacity,
- construction status,
- power source choices,
- and how different developers are handling site-specific constraints.

That makes it useful as a snapshot of how frontier AI capacity gets built in practice.

## The important numbers

- Stargate spans `7` US sites.
- Abilene is already at about `0.6 GW`.
- The full build-out is projected to exceed `9 GW`.
- The report compares that to the peak power demand of New York City.

## The main pattern

The same constraints keep showing up across the sites:

- grid connection delays are painful,
- local communities care about water use,
- and timeline pressure pushes builders toward on-site generation and standardized cooling designs.

The project is therefore a good example of how AI infrastructure decisions are constrained by utility politics and civil engineering, not just by demand for compute.

## Site-by-site takeaway

### Abilene, Texas

The most mature site. It is the only one already operating and serves as the reference point for the rest of the build-out.

### Shackelford County and Dona Ana County

Both are large multi-building campuses that rely on natural gas microgrids to reduce dependence on grid upgrades.

### Milam County

A SoftBank-backed fast-build site with explicit energy generation and storage plans.

### Port Washington and Saline Township

These sites emphasize grid power and low-carbon or battery-supported supply mixes.

### Lordstown

The least specified site, with completion still unknown.

## Why this matters

The deeper point is that frontier AI is becoming an infrastructure race. Model quality still matters, but the ability to stand up gigawatt-scale capacity is now a strategic constraint of its own.

## Bottom line

Stargate is no longer hypothetical. It is a multi-site industrial program that is already consuming land, power planning, and construction capacity, and its final shape will depend on whether the builders can keep the sites moving on schedule.
